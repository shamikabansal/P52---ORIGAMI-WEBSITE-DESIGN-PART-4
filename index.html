Panda

  • Mother pandas keep contact with their cub nearly 100% of the time during their first month
  • Pandas eat consistently for 12-14 hours a day!
  • Newborn pandas are pink!

Camels

  • Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.
  • There are two types of camels: One humped or “dromedary” camels and two humped Bactrian camels.
  • Camels can travel at up to 40 miles per hour – the same as a racehorse!

Teddy Bears

  1. The term bear-hug was first recorded in 1846.
  2. PThe Oxford English Dictionary dates the first use of the term teddy bear to 1906.
  3. Prince Charles, Prince of Wales, allegedly still keeps his teddy bear on his bed, and travels with his teddy bear

Pigeon

  • Pigeons are highly sociable animals. They will often be seen in flocks of 20-30 birds.
  • Pigeons have excellent hearing abilities. They can detect sounds at far lower frequencies than humans are able to, and can thus hear distant storms and volcanoes.
  • Pigeons and humans have lived in close proximity for thousands of years. The first recordings of this date back to Mesopotamis, modern Iraq, in 3000bc.

Chameleon

  • Chameleons have eyes in the backs of their heads
  • Chameleons have extremely powerful tongues
  • Chameleons take a while to hatch, and don’t live very long
